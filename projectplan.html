1. Project Information

Introduction

What is the problem?

Textbooks are very expensive, and there is currently not any system that allows for students to borrow textbooks from each other within the University of Washington. The price of textbooks increases every year if not every quarter. Break on Cost Of Textbooks Unlikely Before Last Bell, 2010, an article in the Washington Post claims that the average cost for textbooks is approximately $700 to $1,100 per academic year. (link )

Some students decide to keep their books at home rather than reselling them like other students. If at some point they decide to finally sell their books, they are often no longer relevant because the professors usually opt for the new versions of textbooks that are used throughout campus. This results in the students ending up selling the books that were bought very expensively to be sold for a fraction of the original cost. Instead of keeping their books at home and collecting dust only to lose the value upon resell at some distant point in the future, students could opt to lend them to their friends or other students.

[a]

While students may share the same major, they do not always know what classes their peers are taking unless they asked. It is easy for the fact to go unknown that a friend may have a current copy of a textbook that is being used in a class that someone else is taking, as the other student may have taken the course in the previous term. So rather than the need to go out and pay for the new copy, or even get it at a lower cost by finding a used copy, students could save this money by simply having a way to find that book being offered for trade or loan by another student.[b]

Solution

We are creating a new web-based application that allows students at the University of Washington to lend or borrow textbooks to and from other students.[c]  A student will not have to buy textbooks so long as there are textbooks to borrow.[d]  This is something that many students will find extremely helpful, and we think it is something that many people would love to use. Students will be able to create their profiles and enter their basic information such as name, major, class standing etc. They can post their book information including title, author, ISBN, publisher, date, and any other relevant information, in order for other students to be able to borrow. Students can also find or search as well as request books to borrow from other students.

The system would be free to all UW students and the benefits that it bears are potentially enormous. Students would have the potential to be able to save thousands of dollars in their time at the UW by using the system to borrow books from other students rather than buying them.

2. Risk and Resource Management

Risk Analysis

  1. Problem: Cannot implement functionality that we planned because of technical incapability (likelihood: medium)
  1. Workaround: The group will try to ask for help from professor, TA , and friends. Each of us know a friend or two who we think can help us with technical difficulties. If we still cannot solve the problems we will probably have to re-scope the project by modifying requirements. Time is very critical so the problem would need to get resolved very quickly
  1. Problem: Member consistently do not finish their assigned tasks on time. (likelihood: low)
  1. Workaround: Talk as a group to solve the problem. The leader will ask the member to be more responsible and work harder.
  2. Workaround: If the problem persists, then professor will be informed. Hopefully, professor will have a solution for us.

*This is a tough situation because everyone does not have much time to work for other member. If the problem does not get resolved, the project could fail.

  1. Problem: Server failure (likelihood: low)
  1. Workaround: We have backups of the actual data and code so we can restore it to another server.
  2. Workaround: We have a second web and database server that we can use right a way if the current server fails.
  3. Workaround: The current server is a virtual machine so we can run it on any hardware setup, even the lab computers.

  1. Problem: PosgreSQL turns out to be complicated. (likelihood: low)
  1. Workaround: Ask knowledgeable peers for tips.
  2. Workaround: Switch to mySQL database. We are more familiar with mySQL and most, if not all of us, have it installed in our student web server. It won’t be a huge problem if we need to switch.

  1. Problem: Design and Implementation phase takes more time than expected.  (likelihood: medium)
  1. Workaround: Work on the weekends[f] .
  2. Workaround: Only implement core features so that the Evaluation and Impact Assessment can proceed as planned.

  1. Problem: A member gets sick and cannot work (likelihood: normal)
  1. Workaround: Other team member will have to put up more time to cover the sick member
  2. Workaround: If everyone will be busy and cannot finish the task, then the group will have to decide what to do and possibly have to delay the deliverable or reduce the functionality of the application. Hopefully this will not happen.
